In May of 2019 I joined Uber to work on a new company wide design system.

I had wanted to implement a design system since my days as the lone designer and developer at ScaleFunder, where I was always looking for ways to be more efficient when building out new products and features. One of the concepts I came across, which at that moment was just starting to coaelsce in the tech vernacular, was this idea of a "design system". I remember redesigning ScaleFunder's admin panel using a very naive design system based on Sketch templates, BEM, and Sass mixins. It was pretty rough. Since then, the tools for creating and managing a design system have evolved quite a bit (React, Figma, etc).

The idea of working on something that unites both design and engineering, in practice and operationally, has always seemed very appealing to me. Design systems are now an integral piece in frontend efficiency, even for small projects. The concept has created clear ways for designer/developer types like me to provide value to an organization.

When the opportunity came up to work on a new system for Uber, I had to give it a shot.

The Base design system spans multiple platforms (Figma, Mobile, Web). My team specifically works on Base Web, the React implementation of Base, as well as more foundational projects like Styletron, a "blazing-fast" CSS-in-JS library. You may notice that both projects are open source- one awesome facet of our work.

Uber has a suprising number of web applications. There are around a thousand interal apps, covering use cases such as mapping, monitoring, and data-modeling, in addition to some fairly critical consumer facing apps you may have heard of: Freight, Eats, and a PWA version of Rides.
